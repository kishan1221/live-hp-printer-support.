How Printers Work

Printers are devices that accept text and graphic output from a computer and transfer the information to paper, usually to standard size sheets of paper. Most printers today are either inkjet or laser printers. Inkjet printers spray tiny droplets of ink directly onto the paper. They're best for photos and detailed color work. Laser printers use a laser beam to produce an image on a drum, which is then transferred to paper using toner and heat — ideal for high-speed, high-volume printing.
